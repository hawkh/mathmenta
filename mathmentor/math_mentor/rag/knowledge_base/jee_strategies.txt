TOPIC: JEE Math – Problem-Solving Strategies & Pitfall Guide
SOURCE: Math Mentor Knowledge Base v1.0

--- GENERAL PROBLEM-SOLVING STRATEGY ---
1. READ CAREFULLY: Identify what is given (data) and what is asked (objective).
2. CLASSIFY: Determine topic (algebra, probability, calculus, linear algebra).
3. RECALL: What formulas/theorems apply to this type?
4. SIMPLIFY: Reduce to standard form before applying formulas.
5. COMPUTE: Show intermediate steps.
6. VERIFY: Substitute answer back; check units, domain, edge cases.
7. SANITY CHECK: Is answer reasonable? Does it satisfy all constraints?

--- TIME MANAGEMENT IN JEE ---
- Quick problems (1-2 min): Direct formula application
- Medium problems (3-4 min): Need to set up + compute
- Hard problems (5-6 min): Novel approach or multi-step chain
- If stuck > 2 min on JEE: mark and move on

--- READING MATHEMATICAL NOTATION ---
"at least one" → use complement: 1 - P(none)
"exactly k" → often binomial P(X=k)
"find the range" → usually means domain constraints + maxima/minima
"prove that" → start from one side, derive the other
"if and only if" → prove both directions
"for all x" → universal statement, find counterexample to disprove
"there exists x" → existential, find one example to prove

--- ALGEBRA PITFALLS ---
1. Squaring both sides introduces extraneous roots → ALWAYS verify roots.
2. log(a+b) ≠ log(a) + log(b)
3. √(a²) = |a|, NOT just a (for negative a)
4. (a+b)² ≠ a² + b² — don't forget 2ab
5. When dividing by (x-a), first check x ≠ a
6. AP/GP: always identify whether it's sum or nth term asked

--- CALCULUS PITFALLS ---
1. d/dx[f(g(x))] requires chain rule — most common error is skipping it
2. Product rule has TWO terms: f'g + fg' — not just f'g'
3. ∫ e^(ax) dx = e^(ax)/a + C — don't forget the 1/a
4. When computing limits, 0·∞ is also indeterminate (rewrite as 0/0 or ∞/∞)
5. For optimization: ALWAYS check both critical points AND endpoints
6. Definite integral area: take |f(x)-g(x)| when curves intersect

--- PROBABILITY PITFALLS ---
1. "With replacement" vs "without replacement" changes everything.
2. P(A∩B) ≠ P(A)·P(B) unless A,B are independent.
3. Conditional probability: P(A|B) — B is the given (denominator).
4. "At least one" → 1 - P(none of them) is almost always easier.
5. For Bayes: systematically list all prior states and their likelihoods.
6. P(drawing red ball) changes after first draw if without replacement.

--- LINEAR ALGEBRA PITFALLS ---
1. AB ≠ BA — matrix multiplication is NOT commutative.
2. If det(A) = 0, the matrix is singular and has NO inverse.
3. det(A+B) ≠ det(A) + det(B)
4. Eigenvalue: solve det(A-λI)=0, not det(A)-λ=0.
5. Zero determinant doesn't mean zero matrix.
6. For system AX=B: check for consistency before solving.

--- SPECIAL NUMBERS & FACTS TO MEMORIZE ---

log₁₀ 2 ≈ 0.301
sin 30° = 1/2, sin 45° = 1/√2, sin 60° = √3/2

--- VERIFICATION CHECKLIST ---
After solving ANY problem:
□ Did I answer what was actually asked?
□ Are all domain restrictions respected?
□ Did I include all cases (both ± in quadratic formula, etc.)?
□ Does the numerical answer have correct sign / magnitude?
□ Can I substitute back to verify?
□ Did I check edge cases (boundaries, zeros, infinity)?

--- COMMON JEE TRAPS ---
1. Integer answer ± 1 off → arithmetic error at the end
2. Forgot to include all combinations in probability (tree diagram helps)
3. Wrong sign in cofactor expansion of determinant
4. Used wrong formula version (e.g., sum of AP vs sum of GP)
5. Misidentified the variable (what's given vs what's unknown)
6. Answer is negative when asked for length/probability → recheck
